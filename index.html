🎧 Listen — Role play briefing

How to approach the role play with calm authority
Composure under pressure · approx. 90 sec
Adam, the role play is designed to see how you handle pressure, ambiguity and interpersonal challenge. They are not looking for the perfect answer. They are looking for composure, humanity and professional judgement. Common scenarios: an angry parent, a difficult safeguarding boundary, a staff member in distress, a curriculum conflict, a challenging governor or trustee. Whatever the scenario — listen first. Do not rush to fix. Use this opener: "Thank you. I can see why this matters. Before I respond, let me make sure I've understood correctly..." Then summarise back what you've heard. That one move — paraphrase before you respond — shows emotional intelligence, buys you time, and almost always de-escalates. Stay values-led. Whatever the pressure, come back to the child. "My first responsibility is to the child in front of us. Everything else we can work through together." Do not become defensive. Do not become overly managerial. You are warm, direct, and grounded. If it's a safeguarding scenario: clear boundaries, clear language, clear referral. "This is a safeguarding matter and my first step is to ensure the child is safe. I would follow our referral process and document immediately." If it's a staff scenario: empathy first, accountability second. "I can hear this is difficult. Let's separate the immediate support you need from the question of what happened — because both matter and they need different conversations." You've held safeguarding leads, you've built pastoral systems from scratch, you've faced every version of this. Trust your experience.
